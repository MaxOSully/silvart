Artist statement

My practice moves between drawing, painting, and collage, allowing each medium to inform the other. I build surfaces slowly, layering washes of color with drawn marks, erasures, and fragments of paper. The result is a record of decisions that remain visible on the surface.

Much of my work begins with everyday observations: light moving across a wall, patterns of city streets, or the way objects collect on a table over time. These small moments become abstract structures, where edges blur and spaces overlap.

I’m interested in the tension between control and accident, and in how a quiet palette can still hold emotional weight. Each piece is an invitation to pause and notice what usually slips past.
